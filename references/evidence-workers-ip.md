# Evidence Workers IP

## Role

"Evidence workers" are anonymous audit-office clerks who handle claims as physical paperwork. They are not mascots, not cute companions, and not a Xiaohei-like black silhouette. They make invisible reasoning feel bureaucratic, material, and inspectable.

## Signature Silhouette

Make the worker recognizable by office-audit geometry, not by cuteness:

- Rectangular stamp-like head or archive-box head, mostly white fill with black outline.
- Tiny square eyes or one inspection slit; no smile, no expressive face.
- Small rectangular body with a hanging ID tag such as `A-07`, `R-12`, or `QA`.
- Thin gloved arms; simple legs; posture like a tired clerk doing precise work.
- One signature tool: red stamp, blue trace spool, orange routing string, permission key, sealed envelope, or paper tray.
- Never use a solid black blob body. Avoid round cute bodies.

## Personality

- They are dry, procedural, and mildly absurd.
- They distrust unsupported claims.
- They treat logs, permissions, proposals, traces, and decisions as physical objects.
- They do not celebrate. They stamp, seal, route, replay, file, and hand off.

## Core Actions

Use one core action per image:

- weigh a claim on a scale
- stamp "已验证" or "待复查"
- sort claims into trays
- patch a leaking workflow pipe
- guard a boundary gate
- pull a trace line through a maze
- carry a tiny evidence brick
- compare before/after samples
- replay a log tape
- place a claim under a glass cover
- hand off a sealed envelope to another worker
- repair a broken agent chain

## Signature Proof Objects

Reusable physical objects:

- evidence bricks
- claim slips
- trace ribbons
- audit stamps
- boundary gates
- log tapes
- receipt drawers
- review trays
- confidence scales
- moat bridges
- black boxes with tiny inspection windows
- sealed envelopes
- version tags
- red rejection pad
- blue replay cassette
- orange routing string
- permission key ring
- transparent evidence sleeve

Objects should look like office artifacts, not fantasy props. A good scene feels like an absurd claims-processing office on a blank page.

## Mandatory Difference From Xiaohei-Like Art

- The worker is mostly white/outline, rectangular, stamp-headed, and tagged.
- The scene centers paperwork mechanics: trays, envelopes, stamps, ledgers, ribbons, tapes, seals.
- Labels often appear as stamps, tags, and sticky notes rather than casual captions.
- The mood is audit-office deadpan, not strange mascot performance.
- The recurring identity is a system of proof handling, not a single black character.

## Bans

- Do not copy "小黑" as a solid black blob with white dot eyes and thin legs.
- Do not turn workers into cute stickers.
- Do not make them stand beside a diagram doing nothing.
- Do not use generic robots unless the user specifically asks for robots.
- Do not overload the scene with many workers. One to three is usually enough.
