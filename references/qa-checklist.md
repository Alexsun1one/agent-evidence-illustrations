# QA Checklist

## Shot List

- Each shot has one claim or mechanism.
- The placement in the article is clear.
- The image earns its space; it is not decorative filler.
- The metaphor is physical and specific.
- Two shots do not reuse the same metaphor unless intentionally forming a series.
- Suggested labels are short and in the article's vocabulary.

## Generated Image

Accept if:

- 16:9 horizontal.
- Pure white background OR premium evidence-object mode with calm paper/desk space.
- Sparse black hand-drawn line art OR refined tactile paper-object illustration with restrained shadows.
- At least 35% empty/quiet space.
- Evidence worker or proof object performs the core action.
- Worker has the distinct audit-office silhouette: rectangular stamp/archive head, ID tag, mostly white fill, thin gloved arms, precise tool.
- Labels feel like stamps, tags, seals, sticky notes, or tray labels rather than generic captions.
- Chinese labels are few and short.
- Red/orange/blue are restrained.
- The image is understandable without becoming a formal diagram.
- Showcase images look like publishable article art, not only a process sketch.
- Native label status is explicit: short labels are readable in-image, intentionally omitted, or externally overlaid only by explicit request.

Regenerate or edit if:

- It looks like a PPT slide, architecture diagram, UI dashboard, or stock vector.
- It uses a generic robot, brain, rocket, cloud, or lightbulb cliche.
- It copies a Xiaohei-like black blob character.
- It looks like generic hand-drawn mascots doing generic tasks.
- It uses construction-worker vibes instead of audit-office paperwork mechanics.
- The worker is just standing nearby.
- The scene is too crowded.
- There is a top-left title or structure label.
- Chinese text is long, garbled, or central to comprehension.
- The image tries to explain every detail in the article.

## Delivery

Report:

- saved file path
- intended placement/use
- which images are production-ready
- which images should be regenerated and why
