# Style DNA

## One Line

White paper, audit-office paperwork, stamp-headed clerks, physical proof objects, sparse Chinese labels, dry product-engineering wit.

## Must

- Use 16:9 horizontal article illustrations.
- Keep a pure white background. No paper texture, beige, gradients, shadows, grids, glass, or cinematic lighting.
- Use black hand-drawn line art with slight wobble. It should feel like a smart engineer drew an absurd audit office on paper, not like polished vector stock art.
- Leave at least 35% blank white space. The central subject should usually occupy 40%-60% of the canvas.
- Use only 3-8 short Chinese labels, each 2-8 characters when possible. Prefer stamp marks, file tags, tray labels, seal labels, or sticky notes over floating captions.
- Make one image explain one claim, one boundary, one workflow, one failure mode, or one proof mechanism.
- Let paperwork mechanics carry the idea. Labels support the drawing; they do not become the drawing.

## Palette

- Black: line art, workers, proof objects, main labels.
- Orange: main path, handoff, movement, state transition.
- Red: risk, broken claim, failing test, warning, final verdict.
- Blue: system state, feedback, trace, assistant/agent signal, secondary context.
- Use red mostly as stamp ink, orange as routing string/path, blue as trace ribbon/replay signal.

Use color sparingly. A good image still works in black and white.

## Texture

The drawing should look handmade but clean: thin pen, no dense shading, no realistic rendering, no 3D, no UI screenshot, no glossy tech poster. The repeatable texture is "blank-page audit office": trays, stamps, sleeves, ledgers, tapes, keys, envelopes, and tagged clerks.

## Hard Bans

- No PPT infographic.
- No formal architecture diagram.
- No complex dashboard UI.
- No cute mascot poster.
- No generic robot, brain, rocket, cloud, lightbulb, or blockchain chain cliche.
- No stock-business people.
- No huge decorative gradient.
- No top-left title like "流程图", "系统架构", "证据链", or "Agent 工作流".
- No full paragraph text inside the image.
- No attempt to explain every node. Keep useful ambiguity.
- No Xiaohei-like solid black mascot silhouette.
- No generic construction workers unless the article literally concerns construction.
